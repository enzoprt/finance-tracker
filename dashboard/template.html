Net worth

Total value across Saxo, LCL, N26, and your Livrets (Livret A / Livret Jeune), converted to EUR. Livrets can't be fetched via any bank API (outside PSD2 scope) — their balance is a manual seed plus detected transfers. Saxo is valued at position-open price, not live market price — see the Allocation tab.

By account

ETF performance

XIRR (money-weighted) accounts for exactly when you deposited or withdrew money — it's your personal real-world return. TWR (time-weighted) ignores deposit timing and is the fair number to compare against a benchmark index, since the benchmark can't "know" when you added cash. Both are computed over the date range shown below.

Return by period

Trailing time-weighted return for each window, computed by comparing the cumulative TWR at the start and end of the window — the same deposit-neutral method used for the headline TWR figure above. A period is only shown once there's enough history behind it.
Monthly returns

Drawdown

How far the portfolio's time-weighted value has fallen from its running peak, at any given date — the standard "pain" measure funds report alongside returns. Computed on TWR, not raw account value, so deposits/withdrawals don't distort it.

Performance by holding

Unrealized P&L per position, from Saxo's own P&L calculation (accurate even without a live market-data feed). Sorted worst to best — the same read used in the AI Overview's "which positions are in the red" analysis.

By geography

Real look-through data via each fund's top ~10 holdings (Yahoo Finance), weighted by that fund's value in your portfolio. Country is inferred from each stock's listing exchange. Since only the top holdings per fund are available (not the full list, e.g. a "World" fund can hold 1,500+ stocks), the uncovered remainder shows as "Other holdings (beyond top 10 per fund)" rather than being silently dropped.

Holdings

All open ETF positions, weighted by value. Values use Saxo's position-open price — this app has no live market-data subscription (trade/v1/infoprices returns "NoAccess"), so weights lag current prices and won't exactly match your unrealized-gain-adjusted portfolio value shown on Overview.

Spending by type

Categorized by keyword-matching the merchant name in each transaction. Purchases that don't match a known merchant keyword fall into "other"; payments routed through PayPal show as "online_payment" since the actual merchant behind PayPal isn't visible in the bank data.

Transaction history

Every LCL and N26 transaction, newest first. "Bank" shows which account it's on; "Category" is the same classification used in the charts above (income, spending, fee, or internal_transfer for moves like a Livret top-up).

Budget

A rolling everyday spending tracker, not tied to any specific trip - pick a preset window or an exact custom range below, and every section on this tab (category breakdown, chart, monthly bars, transaction list) updates to match. Same LCL/N26 transactions as the rest of the dashboard, nothing separate to set up. Useful for daily life as much as for a specific period like the Quebec exchange. "Without transfers" (default) hides detected transfers between your own accounts (e.g. towards a Livret) so they don't inflate "Spent" - switch to "With transfers" to see gross cash movement including those.

Spending by category

Same keyword-based categorization as Cash Flow's "Spending by type" (src/spending.py) - one shared classifier for both tabs. Extend SPENDING_KEYWORDS there as new recurring merchants show up, wherever you are.

Foreign exchange cost

For each foreign-currency card purchase, compares the rate you were actually charged against the ECB reference rate for that date (frankfurter.app). Positive % = you paid more than the ECB rate. Useful to watch during the Quebec semester for EUR→CAD costs.

Anomalies

Automatic checks, nothing more: fees ≥20€, same account/amount/merchant charges within 2 days (possible duplicates — could be legitimate, e.g. two toll crossings), and spending ≥5× your median transaction. Review, don't assume they're errors.
