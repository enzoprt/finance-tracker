AI Overview

Net worth

Total value across Saxo, LCL, N26, and your Livrets (Livret A / Livret Jeune), converted to EUR. Livrets can't be fetched via any bank API (outside PSD2 scope) — their balance is a manual seed plus detected transfers. Saxo is valued at position-open price, not live market price — see the Allocation tab. "After tax on gains" is a rough hypothetical - what you'd have left if you sold every ETF position today, assuming France's 30% flat tax (PFU) on gains (0% on positions currently at a loss, no loss-offset modeled) since the PEA/PEA-PME are both empty and everything sits in the taxable CTO. Not tax advice.

By account

ETF performance

XIRR (money-weighted) accounts for exactly when you deposited or withdrew money — it's your personal real-world return. TWR (time-weighted) ignores deposit timing and is the fair number to compare against a benchmark index, since the benchmark can't "know" when you added cash. Both are computed over the date range shown below.

Return by period

Trailing time-weighted return for each window, computed by comparing the cumulative TWR at the start and end of the window — the same deposit-neutral method used for the headline TWR figure above. A period is only shown once there's enough history behind it.
Monthly returns

Drawdown

How far the portfolio's time-weighted value has fallen from its running peak, at any given date — the standard "pain" measure funds report alongside returns. Computed on TWR, not raw account value, so deposits/withdrawals don't distort it.

Performance by holding

Unrealized P&L per position, from Saxo's own P&L calculation (accurate even without a live market-data feed). Sorted worst to best — the same read used in the AI Overview's "which positions are in the red" analysis.

ETF fees

Each fund's ongoing charges (TER) - deducted from the fund's own price daily by the manager, so it's already fully absorbed into every return shown on this page; you never pay it as a separate line item. This is a different thing from the Fees & FX tab, which tracks real, separate, out-of-pocket broker/bank fees that are NOT absorbed anywhere. Post-tax value is a rough hypothetical - "if you sold everything today" - assuming France's 30% flat tax (PFU) on gains, since the PEA/PEA-PME are both currently empty and every position sits in the taxable account. Not tax advice; verify anything tax-sensitive independently.

By geography

Real look-through data via each fund's top ~10 holdings (Yahoo Finance), weighted by that fund's value in your portfolio. Country is inferred from each stock's listing exchange. Since only the top holdings per fund are available (not the full list, e.g. a "World" fund can hold 1,500+ stocks), the uncovered remainder shows as "Other holdings (beyond top 10 per fund)" rather than being silently dropped.

Holdings

All open ETF positions, weighted by value. Values use Saxo's position-open price — this app has no live market-data subscription (trade/v1/infoprices returns "NoAccess"), so weights lag current prices and won't exactly match your unrealized-gain-adjusted portfolio value shown on Overview.

Budget

A rolling everyday spending tracker, not tied to any specific trip - pick a preset window or an exact custom range below, and every section on this tab (category breakdown, chart, monthly bars, transaction list) updates to match. Same LCL/N26 transactions as the rest of the dashboard, nothing separate to set up. Useful for daily life as much as for a specific period like the Quebec exchange. Three switches control what counts towards "Spent": Rent and Groceries are on by default (real spending); Transfers to your own accounts (e.g. towards a Livret) is off by default so shuffling your own money doesn't inflate the total.

Spending by category

Keyword-based categorization (src/spending.py) - hover or tap a category to see its subcategory split (e.g. Transport → flights / train / public transit / gas / car rental). Extend SPENDING_KEYWORDS there as new recurring merchants show up, wherever you are.

Subscriptions

Every transaction classified as a subscription (streaming, software/cloud) within the selected window, grouped by merchant - so you can see exactly which subscriptions you're paying for, not just a "subscriptions" total.

Net position over time

Cumulative income minus expenses within the selected window - not your total bank balance, which also holds money unrelated to this window.

Monthly income/expense

Income vs. expense per calendar month within the selected window.

Transaction history

Every LCL/N26 transaction within the selected window, newest first.

Fees

Detected via keyword matching: COTISATION / FRAIS / AGIOS / COMMISSION for LCL, "Fee" for N26 (e.g. card fees, foreign transaction fees).

Foreign exchange cost

For each foreign-currency card purchase, compares the rate you were actually charged against the ECB reference rate for that date (frankfurter.app). Positive % = you paid more than the ECB rate. Useful to watch during the Quebec semester for EUR→CAD costs.

Anomalies

Automatic checks, nothing more: fees ≥20€, same account/amount/merchant charges within 2 days (possible duplicates — could be legitimate, e.g. two toll crossings), and spending ≥5× your median transaction. Review, don't assume they're errors.
